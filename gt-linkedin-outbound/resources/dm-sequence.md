# Post-Connection DM Sequence - M1 + M2

You write the 2-message LinkedIn DM sequence that follows a connection acceptance. This is fundamentally different from cold email - the prospect just did something social by accepting. They are slightly warm, but did NOT opt in to a pitch.

## The Sequence

```
M1  - Sent immediately after acceptance (within 2 hours, auto or manual)
M2A - Sent within 24h of THEIR reply (conversation continuation)
M2B - Sent 3-5 days after M1 if NO reply (new angle bump)
```

After M2, **stop.** A third DM rarely improves reply rate and damages account standing. If still no reply, move the prospect to email or pause for 60 days before re-engagement (route to **re-engagement** sub-skill).

## Process

1. **Confirm context** - What's the company/offer/ICP? What signal triggered the connection? Did they engage with content first?
2. **Confirm seniority** - ATL or BTL? Tone diverges sharply.
3. **Pick M1 angle** - Profile-signal based (strongest) > tension-based (when no signal).
4. **Draft M1** - 40-70 words, no pitch, no product name, one open-ended question.
5. **Draft both M2 variants** - M2A (they replied) builds on the conversation. M2B (no reply) opens a NEW angle from scratch.

## Universal DM Rules

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| 40-70 words MAX per message | Mobile readability, conversational rhythm |
| No subject line | DMs don't have one |
| No product/company name in M1 | Triggers the "this is a sale" filter |
| No CTA to book a call in M1 | Too fast - they JUST accepted |
| One question per message | Multiple questions overwhelm and reduce reply rate |
| Never start with a question | Open with an observation or statement |
| No emojis | Signals automation in B2B outbound |
| No "Thanks for connecting!" opener | Most-burned phrase on LinkedIn - instant disconnect |
| No "Just following up" / "Circling back" | Email phrasing in a DM screams template |
| "You" > "I" | Always |

## MESSAGE 1 - Warm Conversation Opener

**Goal:** Get a reply. That's it.

Not pitch. Not qualify. Not book. **Reply.**

If the first DM feels like a cold email pasted into a chat window, the prospect's brain flips to "this person is automated" and you've burned the connection.

### M1 Structure
```
[Opening - observation or statement about THEIR world, 10-15 words, NOT a question]
[1-2 sentences - develop the observation, show you understand their context]
[Soft close - one genuine question or open door, NOT a meeting request]
```

### Profile-Signal Openers (Strongest)

```
"Saw your post about [topic] - [one-sentence genuine reaction]."

"[Company] just [signal]. That shift usually brings [specific challenge] to the surface."

"Your move to [new role] at [company] caught my attention - [observation about that transition]."

"Noticed [team] is hiring [role] at [company]. That phase usually means [related friction]."
```

### Tension-Based Openers (When No Signal Exists)

```
"Most [role]s at [company stage] are dealing with [specific tension] right now."

"[Industry] is going through [shift] - the [function] impact is usually the last thing addressed."

"There's a version of [problem] that shows up consistently for [role]s at [company type]."
```

### M1 Soft Close Options

```
"Curious how you're thinking about [topic] at [company]?"
"Is [challenge] something that's come up for your team?"
"How are you approaching [topic] right now?"
```

### M1 Forbidden Moves

- Mentioning your product, tool, company, or "we"
- Any version of "I wanted to reach out because..."
- Calendar link or "Got 15 min?"
- Multiple questions stacked
- Generic compliments ("Loved your work")
- Long preamble before the actual point

## MESSAGE 2A - They Replied (Conversation Continuation)

**Goal:** Build on what they said. Earn the soft next step.

### M2A Structure
```
[1 sentence - acknowledge what they said specifically (proves you read it)]
[1-2 sentences - add something new: a resource, a data point, a relevant observation]
[Soft CTA - concrete next step at low friction]
```

### M2A CTA Options

```
"Happy to share how [similar company type] approached this - worth a 15-minute call?"
"I have [day] or [day] free if you want to compare notes."
"Sending you something relevant - let me know if useful."
"There's a 1-page breakdown I put together on [exact topic they raised] - want me to send it?"
```

### M2A Tone Rule

Match THEIR rhythm. If they replied in 2 short sentences, you reply in 2 short sentences. If they wrote a thoughtful paragraph, mirror that depth. Most DM replies on LinkedIn are short - DON'T flood them with a 5-paragraph response.

---

## MESSAGE 2B - No Reply (New Angle Bump)

**Goal:** Restart the conversation with a completely different angle. Treat M1 as if it never happened.

### M2B Critical Rule

**Do NOT reference M1.** No "Following up on my last message", no "Just bumping this", no "In case you missed it." These phrases:
- Confirm to the prospect that this is automated outreach
- Highlight that they ignored you (creates social tension)
- Reduce reply rate by 30-50% in tested campaigns

Open M2B as if it were a fresh first message - different angle, different pain, different lens.

### M2B Structure
```
[Opening - completely new angle, different pain or lens, 10-15 words]
[1-2 sentences - develop the new angle briefly]
[Value offer - a resource or insight they can use now, no ask attached]
[Optional soft CTA - only if it fits naturally]
```

### M2B New Angle Options

| If M1 used... | M2B should pivot to... |
|---|---|
| Their team's operational pain | Their personal credibility / career lens |
| Current pain | Market or timing trigger (funding, hiring, regulatory) |
| The problem | A resource that helps regardless of your product |
| Specific data point | A counterintuitive industry insight or framework |

### M2B CTA Options

```
"No pressure - just thought this might be useful given [their context]."
"Worth a quick conversation if [topic] is on your radar?"
"Happy to share more if relevant - [day] or [day] work?"
```

## Value Prop Rotation (Across the Sequence)

Never repeat the same angle:

| Touch | Angle |
|---|---|
| Connection request (note or no note) | Establish context / earn the connection |
| M1 | Their world / a pain or observation about them |
| M2A | Build on what they said + soft offer |
| M2B (no reply) | NEW angle - different pain, different lens, different framing |

If M1 was about "the complexity of scaling SDR teams", M2B might pivot to "what hiring data shows about [industry] this quarter" - different entry point entirely.

## ATL vs BTL DM Differences

| Dimension | ATL (VP/C-Suite) | BTL (Manager/IC) |
|---|---|---|
| Word count | 40-50 max | 50-70 |
| Opening move | Strategic observation about their world | Hyper-specific daily friction |
| What they respond to | Insight that reframes something they think they know | Recognition that someone gets their daily reality |
| Tone | Peer-to-peer, brief, direct | Practitioner, specific, grounded |
| Time to ask | M2 only - never M1 | M2 |
| What kills it | Operational language ("save 3 hours/week") → instant delegation | Strategic language ("revenue impact") → too abstract |

## Examples

### Example 1 - VP RevOps at a Series B SaaS company (signal: posted about forecasting)

**M1**
```
Saw your post on the gap between forecast accuracy and pipeline hygiene.

Most VPs I talk to fix one or the other - rarely both. Usually because the data 
sources don't agree on what counts as "stage 3."

Curious how you're thinking about it at [Company]?
```

**M2A (they replied: "Yeah it's a constant battle, we're trying to fix the source-of-truth issue right now")**
```
That source-of-truth piece is usually where it breaks - in our experience the issue 
isn't the tool, it's that no one owns the definition of stage progression.

Pulled together a 1-pager on how Series B teams structure that ownership. Want me 
to send it over?
```

**M2B (no reply, sent 4 days after M1)**
```
Different angle - Q1 board cycles are exposing forecasting accuracy issues across 
[industry] in a way Q4 didn't.

The pattern is consistent: VPs with strong rep-level forecasts and weak roll-up 
discipline. The fix is usually owned at director level, not rep level.

Worth a quick conversation if forecasting prep is on your Q2 radar?
```

### Example 2 - Head of Demand Gen, hired 4 months ago (no specific post signal)

**M1**
```
Most Heads of Demand Gen 4-6 months in are running into the same wall - the campaigns 
the previous person built can't be killed yet (political), but they're underperforming 
the new bets.

The first 6 months are mostly defending what's already there.

How's that playing out for you at [Company]?
```

**M2A (they replied)**
```
Recognized that exact dynamic. The teams I see handle it well usually pick ONE 
inherited program to publicly improve - gives you cover to retire the rest quietly.

Happy to share how a few [industry] teams structured that. Tuesday or Thursday work 
for a 20-min call?
```

**M2B (no reply)**
```
[Industry] CMO benchmark dropped last week - average CAC up 31% YoY, demand gen 
budgets flat. The implication for your role is messy.

Most Heads who hit number this year aren't running more programs - they're killing 
3 and tripling down on 1.

Want the breakdown? No pitch attached.
```

### Example 3 - Senior Engineer / Tech Lead, BTL persona (signal: certification)

**M1**
```
Noticed the recent [certification] cert - that one usually means the team is 
about to make some structural decisions.

Most senior engineers I talk to in that phase are stuck deciding whether to 
refactor the monolith or just keep patching it.

Where are you landing on that at [Company]?
```

**M2A (they replied)**
```
Refactor camp usually wins long-term but loses politically. The teams that make 
it work tie the refactor to a specific business outcome - not a tech debt argument.

Got a writeup on how a [similar company] team made the case for their CTO. Want it?
```

**M2B (no reply)**
```
Different lens - most engineering leads underestimate how much the [framework / tool 
they're certified in] changes the team hiring profile.

Talent pool tightens, comp expectations shift, and the timing of when you adopt it 
ends up mattering more than whether you do.

Useful framework on this if you want it.
```

## Multi-Channel Coordination

If the same prospect is also being emailed:
- **LinkedIn M1 angle ≠ Email 1 angle** - never repeat the same hook on both channels
- **If they reply on LinkedIn → pause email immediately**
- **If they reply on email → still send LinkedIn M1** (different conversational thread)
- **LinkedIn leads on tone, email leads on depth** - DMs stay short and warm; email can carry more substance

## State-Aware Branching (Conditional Logic)

A linear M1 → M2 → stop sequence is the GT default - but in practice, well-built campaigns use **conditional branching** based on what the prospect did after acceptance. HeyReach (and Expandi, La Growth Machine) all expose this as if/then logic at the campaign level. Same idea works manually for personal-profile campaigns.

The four post-acceptance states, with the right action for each:

| State | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Replied to M1** | Any reply | Exit sequence. Move to live conversation in Unibox. M2A is sent within 24h, but the rest of the automation stops. |
| **Profile viewed, no reply** | Their view appears in your visitor log within 7 days, no DM reply | Send M2B with a different angle. Profile views are a real engagement signal - they're curious. |
| **Connected, totally silent** | No view, no reply, no engagement on your content for 5-7 days | Send M2B with a NEW angle. After M2B → stop. |
| **Replied once, then stalled** | One reply, then 7+ days silence | One thoughtful contextual follow-up referencing the prior exchange. Then stop. See `copywriting/re-engagement.md`. |

The "active but silent" pattern is the dangerous middle ground - they're posting and engaging on LinkedIn but ignoring your DM. The reflex is to send another nudge. The right move is **don't**. Wait for a new external signal (a relevant post, a role change, engagement with your content) before re-engaging. See the full 4-State Follow-up Model in `copywriting/re-engagement.md`.

### Why "Active but silent" gets one nudge max

LinkedIn is socially visible. Email persistence works because the inbox is impersonal - the sender is just another subject line. LinkedIn DMs sit next to the prospect's colleagues and friends in the same notification stream. Multiple unanswered nudges create social pressure and the dynamic flips from "do I want this?" to "how do I make this stop?" Reply rate craters and the sender profile gets quietly downgraded by the prospect's mental model.

Source: HeyReach's analysis of the 4-state model - see `resources/heyreach-knowledge-base.md`.

## Benchmarks to Hit

| Stage | Target |
|---|---|
| M1 reply rate | 15-25% (good), 30%+ (great) |
| M2A reply rate (continuation) | 50-70% - they already replied to M1 |
| M2B reply rate (no-reply bump) | 8-15% - adds 30-50% lift on top of M1 replies |
| Total sequence reply rate | 20-30% (good), 35%+ (great) |
| Reply → meeting conversion | 20-35% |

If M1 reply rate is below 10%, the issue is almost always:
1. Connection list quality (wrong ICP)
2. M1 opener (too generic / too pitchy / mentions product)
3. The sender's profile (looks like a salesperson, not a peer)

Diagnose in that order.
