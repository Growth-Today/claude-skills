# B2B vs B2C on Meta — What Does NOT Transfer (read before applying any generic Meta advice)

~90% of Meta Ads content, benchmarks, and skills on the open web are **B2C/DTC/e-commerce**. Applying them to a B2B account is the most common way to waste budget. This is the GT guardrail: when you read a generic Meta tactic, run it through this filter first. **Never import a DTC/e-commerce benchmark or tactic into a B2B account — not now, not later.**

---

## The seven things that don't transfer

1. **Judge on pipeline/SQL — not ROAS.** DTC optimizes to purchase ROAS. B2B has no purchase at click time; ROAS is meaningless until deals close months later. Optimize and report on **cost per SQL and pipeline created** (via offline conversions), never raw ROAS or Instant-Form CPL.
2. **Cheap leads are the trap, not the goal.** Meta will flood you with low-cost leads. DTC counts that as a win; in B2B it's a cost — sales ignores unqualified leads. Feed CRM outcomes back so Meta optimizes to **deals, not lead volume**.
3. **No catalog / Advantage+ Shopping / commerce.** These are e-commerce products. Out of scope for B2B — do not use. (Advantage+ **Sales/Leads** can be tested for B2B; **Shopping** cannot.)
4. **Frequency & creative economics differ.** DTC frequency-to-conversion-rate rules assume impulse purchase. B2B has a buying committee and a long cycle — a "too high" DTC frequency may be fine for B2B considered purchase; don't copy DTC frequency caps or "3–5 day creative burnout" economics wholesale. Judge fatigue on **your** CTR/lead-quality trend.
5. **First-party + offline is mandatory, not optional.** DTC can lean on Pixel purchases. B2B must run **CAPI + offline conversions from the CRM** (see `crm-attribution.md`) or the algorithm optimizes blind.
6. **Interest-stacking / day-parting / lookalike-refresh cadences** from DTC playbooks are tuned to consumer impulse and high conversion volume — treat as untested hypotheses in B2B, not rules. Custom audiences from first-party data beat interest targeting for B2B relevance.
7. **Benchmarks are not shared.** DTC CPMs/CPLs/ROAS bands do not apply. Use **B2B** ranges only, and treat even those as conditional (objective, geography, ICP, maturity). Meta is a **volume / assist** channel for B2B, not the last-click revenue engine (LinkedIn typically carries higher-intent conversions).

## B2B SaaS tooling worth naming
- **HubSpot** — native Meta integration; lifecycle → offline conversions.
- **Dreamdata** — account-based multi-touch attribution + Meta offline sync; publishes B2B benchmarks.
- **Metadata.io** — B2B paid-social automation (MetaMatch identity graph) for Meta + LinkedIn targeting and pipeline attribution.
- (For LinkedIn ABM engagement tracking, ZenABM — but it does **not** track Meta.)

**Rule of thumb:** if a tactic's success metric is ROAS, AOV, or purchases, it's DTC — adapt it to pipeline/SQL or drop it.
