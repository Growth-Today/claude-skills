# Meta Ads Creative Fatigue

Creative fatigue is the most common reason a working Meta account suddenly drops off. Audiences burn out fast — even on winning creative. Catching it early (and outrunning it with volume) is a core B2B Meta discipline, and it is exactly the kind of thing the competitor's "fatigue monitor" flags. This skill is the strategy behind that signal.

## Instructions

1. Watch the two signals together: rising frequency + falling CTR
2. Refresh proactively, do not wait for performance to crater
3. Run a rolling refresh cadence keyed to spend level
4. Distinguish creative fatigue from audience saturation
5. Pull refreshes from your creative library (see creative)

## The Fatigue Signal

The clear tell is **rising frequency paired with declining CTR**. Neither alone is conclusive; together they mean the audience has seen the ad too often and is tuning it out.

| Signal | Healthy | Warning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Frequency (cold prospecting, 7 days) | 1.5-2.5 | 3.0-4.0+ |
| CTR vs baseline | stable | drops ~20%+ |
| CPM | stable | climbing |
| CTR decay over 2 weeks | gradual | can drop ~40% on repeated creative |

## Refresh Cadence

- **Trigger:** CTR drops ~20%+ from baseline, OR frequency exceeds ~3-3.5 on cold audiences.
- **High-spend creatives** ($300+/day campaigns) show fatigue every 2-4 weeks. Do not wait for the crater.
- **Volume to outrun it:** add 3-5 new creative variations per week at meaningful spend. Run 4-5 ads per ad set and rotate: after ~2 weeks pause the lowest performer, introduce a fresh one. This rolling cycle keeps the ad set healthy without a hard reset.
- **Cheapest refresh:** swap the first 3 seconds (the hook), keep the body. Fastest way to revive a fatiguing winner.

## Fatigue vs Audience Saturation

- **Creative fatigue:** same people, same ad, too many times → refresh creative.
- **Audience saturation / oversaturation:** the audience itself is too small or fully exhausted → expand the audience (broader, new lookalike, new layer) or exclude past engagers and bring fresh prospects in. The competitor's "fatigue monitor" is really watching for this — frequency climbing because the pool is too small.
- Diagnose which before acting: small audience + high frequency = saturation; large audience + high frequency on one ad = creative fatigue.

## Proactive Discipline

A disciplined creative production process (the library system in creative.md) is what makes proactive refreshes possible. If you only make new creative when performance craters, you are always a step behind the fatigue curve. Plan the next batch before you need it.

## Examples

Example 1: "How do I know when to refresh Meta creative?"
→ Watch rising frequency + falling CTR together. Refresh when CTR drops ~20% from baseline or frequency exceeds ~3-3.5 on cold audiences. At $300+/day add 3-5 new variants weekly.

Example 2: "My frequency is climbing fast on a small audience."
→ That is saturation, not just creative fatigue. Expand the audience (broader / new lookalike / new layer) or refresh the pool by excluding past engagers, alongside new creative.
