# LinkedIn Content Strategy: Hooks, Frameworks & Profile Optimization

## Hook Writing Techniques

The hook is text visible before the "see more" fold — ~210 characters on mobile (2-3 lines). This is the most important element of any LinkedIn post.

> **⚠️ PRIMARY REFERENCE:** For GT's current hook system, always load `{SKILL_BASE}/.claude/skills/hooks/gt-SKILL.md` first. It contains the full performance-ranked hook table (3,156-post dataset), GT-specific hook templates per funnel stage, and GT Bank number integration. The formulas below are supplementary reference only.

### Hook Types — Ranked by Performance (3,156-post dataset)

| Hook Type | Avg Reactions | Avg Comments | Best Funnel Stage |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Number lead** ("37 triggers...", "[X] meetings...") | **400** | 98 | ToF/BoF resource posts |
| **Contrarian** ("Stop...", "Everyone...", "Don't...") | 345 | 99 | MoF hot takes |
| **Death declaration** ("R.I.P.", "X is dead") | 332 | 384 | MoF contrarian |
| **News/breaking** ("Just...", "BREAKING:") | 287 | 106 | ToF/MoF trend riding |

| **Personal story** ("I...", "We...", "My...") | 241 | **155** | MoF/BoF trust building |
| **Build showcase** ("Built...", "Created...") | 220 | 129 | MoF system reveals |
| **Question hook** ("How do...?", "What if...?") | 208 | 54 | ToF curiosity plays |

| **Engagement bait** ("Comment your...", "Tag...") | 128 | **170** | BoF lead capture |

**Key insight:** Number-lead hooks dominate reach. Personal story hooks drive the most comments (155 avg). For pipeline, personal stories outperform clickbait — prioritise when MoF discussion or BoF intent is the goal.

### GT Hook Rules

1. First line must be **under 15 words**
2. **Start with the result or bold claim** — never context
3. **Specific numbers beat vague claims** — "[X] meetings" (use your real figure) not "hundreds of meetings"
4. **Never start AND end with a question** — pick one
5. Always use your own real numbers from your own records

### GT Hook Templates by Funnel Stage

**ToF — maximize reach:**
- "Everyone is talking about [X]. Here is what it means for your GTM."
- "[Number] of the best [resource type] for [outcome]."
- "BREAKING: [Tool/Model] just [changed something]. Here is what GTM teams need to know."

**MoF — maximize C/R ratio:**
- "We generated [specific number] [result]. Here is the exact system."
- "How it started: [bad state with number]. How it is going: [good state with number]."
- "[Common belief] is wrong. Here is why."
- "We built [specific thing] for [specific context]. Here is how it works."

**BoF — maximize intent signals:**
- "[Number] of the best [resource]. Comment [KEYWORD] and I will send it over."
- "We generated [X] [result] for a [client type]. Here is what we did step by step."

### Hook Don'ts

- Do NOT start with "I'm excited to announce..." — generic, low engagement
- Do NOT use clickbait the post body doesn't deliver on
- Do NOT start with hashtags or @mentions
- Do NOT bury the hook after a preamble or context
- Do NOT use: leverage, unlock, game-changer, synergy, paradigm, revolutionary
- Do NOT start AND end with a question — pick one position

## Storytelling Frameworks for LinkedIn

> **⚠️ PRIMARY REFERENCE:** The production post structures used by GT are the **6 formulas in the storytelling sub-skill** (System Reveal, Trend Explainer, Resource Drop, Contrarian Take, Journey Post, Tool Comparison). Those are what to use when writing any GT LinkedIn post.
>
> The frameworks below — AIDA, PAS, BAB — are **theoretical backstory only**. They explain the underlying logic of why structured posts work, but they are not production templates. A post written from AIDA labels produces the wrong output: no Step 1/2/3 structure, no ↳ arrows, no mandatory P.S., no data-backed hook, longer sentences, no whitespace discipline.
>
> Use the storytelling sub-skill for writing. Use this section for understanding the theory behind it.

### AIDA / PAS / BAB — Theoretical Background

These three frameworks describe the psychological flow of a persuasive post. They are the academic ancestor of GT's 6 formulas.

**AIDA** (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action): Grab attention, build interest, create desire for the solution, drive action. In GT's System Reveal formula, the hook = Attention, the credibility line = Interest, the Step 1/2/3 body = Desire, the CTA + P.S. = Action. The difference: GT's version is explicit, numbered, and data-backed.

**PAS** (Problem → Agitate → Solution): Name the pain, make it worse, present the fix. In GT's Contrarian Take formula, the opening claim = Problem, the "here's what's actually happening" evidence = Agitation, the "here's what actually works" section = Solution. The difference: GT's version uses ↳ arrows, specific numbers, and never ends without a P.S.

**BAB** (Before → After → Bridge): Show the painful starting point, show the good outcome, explain how to get there. In GT's Journey Post formula ("How it started / How it's going"), Before = the starting state with specifics, After = the result with a number, Bridge = the numbered lessons. The difference: GT's version has mandatory whitespace every 2-3 lines and always ends with a P.S.

### Storytelling Rules (GT Version)

1. **Specificity beats generality**: "[X] meetings in 2025" (use your real figure) not "many meetings"
2. **One post = one idea**: Never cover multiple topics
3. **7-word median line, 20-word hard ceiling**: Not "6th-grade level" — a precise structural target from 3,156-post dataset analysis
4. **Lead with result, not context**: Never start with background or setup
5. **Step 1/2/3 + ↳ for all process content**: The reader must see the structure in the post itself, not just feel it
6. **Every post ends with P.S.**: 63% of top-performing posts in the dataset have one — it is not optional
7. **"We" for team posts, "I" for personal story posts**: Consistent throughout the post

## Profile Optimization

### Headline Formulas (220 characters max)

**Formula 1**: "I help [audience] [achieve result]"
- "I help B2B SaaS companies generate pipeline through cold outreach"

**Formula 2**: "Role | Niche Authority + Proof"
- "Head of Growth @ Acme | Helped 3 startups reach $10M ARR"

**Formula 3**: "[Role] | [What you do] | [Social proof/CTA]"
- "Fractional CMO | Revenue-focused marketing for B2B | Former HubSpot"

**Formula 4**: "Niche Keyword + Differentiator"
- "The only LinkedIn strategist who guarantees results"

**Best Practices:**
- Lead with what you do for others, not job title alone
- Include relevant keywords for LinkedIn search
- Avoid buzzwords: "visionary," "guru," "ninja," "thought leader"
- Use pipe "|" or bullet "·" to separate phrases

### About Section Framework (2,600 chars max)

```
[HOOK - First 2 lines visible before "see more"]
Compelling statement about who you help and what makes you different.

[THE PROBLEM YOU SOLVE - 2-3 sentences]
Paint the pain your audience feels.

[YOUR APPROACH - 3-5 sentences]
How you solve the problem. Your unique methodology.

[PROOF - 3-5 bullet points]
- Specific results achieved
- Notable clients or companies
- Relevant certifications
- Media features or speaking

[WHO I WORK WITH - 2-3 sentences]
Describe ideal client clearly so they self-identify.

[CTA - 1-2 sentences]
Exact next step. DM? Book a call? Email?
Email: your@email.com
```

### Featured Section (Top of Profile)

**Priority order:**
1. Lead magnet / free resource (drives email list)
2. Best-performing LinkedIn post (social proof)
3. Case study / testimonial (proof of results)
4. Newsletter (subscriber growth)
5. Media appearance (authority)

Tips: Max 3-5 items, custom thumbnails, most important = leftmost, update monthly.

### Banner Design
- Dimensions: **1584 x 396 pixels** (4:1 ratio)
- Keep critical text in center (edges cropped on mobile)
- Communicate: who you are, what you do, value prop
- Include: tagline, website URL, key offering, or social proof

### Social Proof Elements

1. **Recommendations**: 5-10+ from clients/colleagues with specific prompts
2. **Skills endorsements**: Pin top 3 relevant skills, aim for 99+
3. **Activity section**: Consistent, high-quality posting = trust
4. **Experience descriptions**: Include metrics ("Grew team from 5 to 25, +340% revenue")
5. **Follower count**: >5,000 signals authority
6. **Custom URL**: Claim linkedin.com/in/yourname

## Key Takeaways

1. **Saves are the new likes** — create content worth bookmarking
2. **Dense single images dominate** — invest in single image infographics / cheat sheets (designed in Claude Design), not carousels
3. **First 90 minutes matter most** — be present and engaged after posting
4. **Hook in 210 characters** — first line determines if anyone reads the rest
5. **3-4 posts/week** — sweet spot for growth without burnout
6. **Comments are a growth channel** — 15+ meaningful comments daily
7. **Newsletters compound** — every subscriber = guaranteed notification
8. **Niche > broad** — algorithm rewards topic consistency
9. **Vulnerability + insight = engagement** — failures + professional lesson
10. **Profile = landing page** — optimize as conversion funnel
