## The Default Structure: Step Framework

For any process, workflow, or system reveal — this is the GT default:

```
[HOOK — from hooks sub-skill]

[1-line credibility or context]

Here's [what we built / the exact system / how it works]:

Step 1: [Action label]
↳ [Specific detail — tool, method, output]
↳ [What happens next — 1 sentence]

Step 2: [Action label]
↳ [Specific detail]
↳ [What happens next]

Step 3: [Action label]
↳ [Specific detail]
↳ [Result or output]

The result: [Specific metric]

[CTA — from cta sub-skill]

P.S. [Mandatory — from cta sub-skill]
```

**When to use:** Workflow reveals, automation builds, client case studies, "here's how we do X" posts. This covers ~60% of GT MoF content.

## 6 Formulas — Choose Based on Funnel Stage

### Formula 1: System Reveal (MoF — highest consistency)
Use for: Clay workflows, automation builds, campaign systems
Structure: Bold result → credibility line → Step 1/2/3 with ↳ → "The result:" → CTA → P.S.
Performance: 200-600 reactions · 500-2,000+ comments
Character target: 1,800-2,500

### Formula 2: Trend Explainer (ToF — highest reach ceiling)
Use for: AI news, new model releases, industry shifts with GTM angle
Structure: Trending topic → "Here's what it means for your GTM" → Visual (required) → 3-5 ↳ points → soft close → P.S. (follow type)
Performance: 1,000-16,000+ reactions
Character target: 1,000-1,800
**Visual is mandatory for ToF posts. Text-only ToF consistently underperforms.**

### Formula 3: Resource Drop (BoF — highest conversion)
Use for: Lead magnets, template giveaways, trigger lists
Structure: Specific result → what the resource is → 3-5 ↳ teaser items → comment-gate CTA → P.S. (service signal)
Performance: 200-1,300 reactions · 400-3,000 comments (intent signals)
Character target: 800-1,500

### Formula 4: Contrarian Take (MoF — high engagement per impression)
Use for: Hot takes, "X is dead", industry myths
Structure: "[Belief] is wrong." → acknowledge common view → "Here's what's actually happening:" → ↳ evidence → "Here's what actually works:" → ↳ alternatives → proof point → "Over to you:" question → P.S.
Performance: 150-800 reactions · high comment quality (debates)
Character target: 1,000-1,800

### Formula 5: Journey Post (MoF/BoF — trust building)
Use for: Agency milestones, client wins, building in public, "how it started/how it's going"
Structure: "How it started: [specific bad state]" → "How it's going: [specific good state]" → 3-5 numbered lessons with ↳ detail → closing conviction statement → P.S.
Performance: 200-2,700 reactions · highest comment quality
Character target: 1,200-2,000
**Text-only acceptable here. Personal vulnerability + specific results = trust.**

### Formula 6: Tool Comparison (MoF — high save rate)
Use for: Tech stack reveals, head-to-head comparisons
Structure: "I tested X tools." → "Here's what I found:" → Tool 1: ↳ verdict → Tool 2: ↳ verdict → Tool 3: ↳ verdict → "Overall winner for [use case]:" → "Over to you:" → P.S.
Performance: 200-1,000 reactions · moderate comments
Character target: 1,500-2,500

## Structural Rules (Non-Negotiable)

**Sentence length:**
- Median: 7 words per line
- Hook lines: 9 words
- Hard ceiling: 20 words per sentence
- 75% of lines should be 4-14 words

**Paragraphs:**
- 1-2 sentences max, then blank line
- No exceptions — no wall-of-text posts

**Arrow usage:**
- ↳ for sub-points under a step or category (nested info)
- → for action items, flows, or processes (sequential)
- Never use bullet points (- or •) — always arrows

**Bold unicode:**
- 1-3 key phrases per post maximum
- Use for the most important insight, result, or term
- Never use for decoration

**Emojis:**
- 0-2 per post maximum
- Only structural: 👇 before CTA, numbered sections 1️⃣ 2️⃣
- Never decorative mid-sentence

## Storytelling Rules

1. **One post = one idea** — never combine two topics
2. **Specificity beats generality** — "4.7% reply rate" beats "better results"
3. **Lead with the result, not the story** — "We generated 40 meetings. Here's how." not "Here's a story about how we tried something new..."
4. **Show the system, not the outcome** — explain the steps, not just the win
5. **"We" for agency/team posts, "I" for personal stories** — be consistent throughout
6. **Active voice** — "We built" not "A system was built"
7. **Name tools directly** — Clay, Instantly, n8n, HubSpot, Apollo — not "our CRM" or "our automation platform"

## Transition Patterns (Use These)

When moving between sections:

1. **"Here's what we did:"** / **"Here's the exact system:"** / **"Here's how it works:"** — standard reveal bridge (349x in top posts)
2. **"Step 1: / Step 2: / Step 3:"** — numbered walkthrough (173x)
3. **"Instead of"** — contrarian pivot (176x)
4. **"The result:"** — payoff reveal (27x)
5. **"Over to you:"** — closer/discussion opener (216x)
6. **"Think of it as"** — analogy bridge (19x)
7. **"What this means for you:"** — relevance bridge (11x)
8. **"Simply put,"** — complexity reducer
9. **"But here's the thing:"** — tension builder
10. **"Most people [don't realize / miss / skip]"** — pain opener

## Output Format

When structuring a post body:
1. Name which formula you're using and why
2. Write the full body with proper whitespace, ↳ arrows, and bold unicode
3. Do not write the hook (that's the hooks sub-skill) or P.S./CTA (that's the cta sub-skill)
4. Keep paragraph structure tight — 1-2 sentences, then line break
5. Flag the character count estimate
