## Format Selection by Objective

| Format | Best For | Key Metric |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Single Image** | Brand awareness, lead capture | CTR, conversions |
| **Video** | Storytelling, demos, testimonials | 10-15c/view, completion rate |
| **Carousel** | Feature showcase, use cases | Per-card engagement |
| **Thought Leader Ads** | Credibility, authority, reach | 2.68% median CTR, $2.29 CPC (ZenABM 2026) |
| **Document Ads** | Whitepapers, case studies | 6-7%+ CTR achievable |
| **Conversation Ads** | Lead gen, interactive journeys | ~$1/send |
| **Text/Spotlight** | Low-cost remarketing | Low CPM |

## Visual Best Practices

- **Non-ad-like ads** — blend into feed, reference everyday concepts → can 3x CTR
- **Square images** (1200x1200) outperform rectangular
- **Moving elements** (GIFs/animations) increase clicks
- **Real customer photos** > polished stock photography
- **Maximize screen real estate** for scroll-stopping

## Thought Leader Ads (14 Strategies)

Top performers: Problem + Solution posts, customer case studies, product demos, event promotions, industry thought leadership.
- Non-employee influencers often outperform employee content
- Posts with 2-3% organic CTR become top-performing ads
- Budget: $300-500/month, $100-150 per post test

### TLA performance & creative rules (ZenABM 2026)

- Median TLA CTR is 2.68% at $2.29 CPC — 6.4x higher CTR than single image, ~4.6x more landing-page clicks per dollar. "Good" is 2.0%+; below 2% means the creative is underperforming for the format.
- Average dwell time 6.63 seconds (highest of any format).
- Two patterns separate top TLAs from average ones:
  - **65% of the highest-CTR TLAs use first-person "I" voice** — literal personal storytelling, not "we / our company."
  - **75% of top TLAs place the link in the bottom 25% of the post text** — the post does the selling, the link just continues it. Counterintuitive vs "above the fold" thinking, but the data is clear.
- Other top-performer traits: open with a concrete statement (not a question), one data point or unexpected finding in the first two lines, keep the post ~800-1,200 characters, and make it read like something the person would post organically.
- **Do not optimise TLAs for CTR alone.** CTR negatively correlates with pipeline (rho = -0.170). Measure TLAs on CTR + dwell time for awareness, but judge the program on pipeline. Pair TLAs (reach) with single image ads (conversion).

### Sequenced TLAs / Story Arcs (run a series, not a single post)

A single boosted post is a test, not a program. The higher-performing pattern is a **Story Arc**: 5-7 posts delivered in sequence to the *same* target accounts, each building on the last. Sequencing beats random one-off promotion because B2B buying cycles are long and familiarity compounds — the same decision-maker seeing post 1, then 3, then 5 over a few weeks moves from "never heard of them" to "I keep seeing them."

Rules for a Story Arc:
- **Never run just one post as a campaign.** Run 4-6 variants minimum. This gives you comparison data and nurtures the audience with multiple angles instead of one.
- **Order the posts deliberately** — e.g. problem → contrarian take → proof/case study → method → offer. The arc tells a story; the audience is fixed, the message progresses.
- **Hold the audience tight** (50,000-500,000, or a named ABM account list). A sequence only compounds if the same people see the whole arc.
- **Give it 14+ days minimum per arc.** LinkedIn's delivery needs time to optimise and the buying cycle is long.
- Pull the arc from posts that already worked organically; sequencing amplifies what resonated, it does not rescue weak content.

### Who TLAs are economically right for

TLAs are not for everyone. The format earns its keep for **B2B companies at roughly $1M+ ARR targeting enterprise or defined ABM accounts**, where relationship-building with a known buying committee is the job. Smaller companies, or anyone without an active executive voice to sponsor, usually get better early ROI from standard sponsored content to build basic awareness first, then layer TLAs once there is an organic content foundation and a credible person to run them from. No active poster, no TLA program — the format depends on content that reads like the person would have posted it anyway.

## Refresh Cadence

- **Every 4-6 weeks** — refresh creatives
- **Monthly** — small retargeting audiences
- **CTR < 0.45%** — immediate creative refresh needed
- **Frequency > 3/week/person** — fatigue risk

## Key Principles

- **Content repurposing first** — audit podcast clips, webinar recordings, demos, testimonials before creating new
- **Keep Document Ads ungated** — broader reach + retargeting pool building
- **Always add subtitles to video** — auto-play is muted
- **Square format** for images and video

## TLA Anti-Patterns (what kills performance)

- **Using TLAs for a hard sell.** The format works because it feels authentic. Turn it into a direct-response ad and you lose the only advantage it has.
- **Targeting too broadly.** A 5M-person audience burns budget with no meaningful impact. Stay 50,000-500,000, or a named account list.
- **Running one post instead of a sequence.** One post is a test. Build a Story Arc of 4-6+ variants.
- **Killing the campaign too early.** Give it 14+ days before judging — delivery needs to optimise and B2B cycles are long.
- **Ignoring comments on the sponsored post.** Every reply extends the conversation and lifts visibility. Respond.
- **Tracking clicks only.** Set up conversion tracking and watch pipeline influence; clicks alone never justify the spend.
