# B2B Negative Keyword Library

A starting library of common B2B negatives by category. Apply universal buckets as account-level shared lists; tailor industry and campaign-level negatives per account. Always check conversion data before adding high-volume terms, and audit monthly for over-negation.

A pre-launch list covering the obvious buckets is essential before a new campaign enters the learning phase, so early spend does not anchor Smart Bidding to junk.

## Bucket 1 — Employment (apply as phrase negatives, account level)
job, jobs, career, careers, hiring, hire, salary, salaries, recruit, recruiter, recruitment, internship, intern, vacancy, vacancies, employment, "work from home", resume, cv, "job description", apprenticeship, "entry level"

Job-seeker queries are the single largest source of wasted B2B spend. Negate account-wide unless you are actively recruiting.

## Bucket 2 — Price / low-intent (review per vertical; premium brands)
free, freeware, cheap, cheapest, low cost, "low cost", discount, coupon, promo, "promo code", deal, bargain, budget, inexpensive, affordable, "for free"

Caution: do NOT add "free" as a broad negative if you offer "free trial", "free demo", or "free consultation". Use exact/phrase on the specific junk variations.

## Bucket 3 — DIY / education / research
"how to", tutorial, tutorials, "what is", "what are", definition, meaning, examples, example, template, templates, pdf, ppt, "open source", diy, "do it yourself", course, courses, certification, class, classes, training, guide, "for dummies", wiki, wikipedia, learn, study

## Bucket 4 — Competitor / comparison (campaign-dependent)
Add competitor brand names as phrase negatives to NON-brand campaigns (and to brand campaigns) unless running a deliberate conquest campaign. Phrase-match brand exclusions catch variations better than exact.
review, reviews, "vs", versus, comparison, alternative, alternatives, "best", "top 10" (until brand/comparison campaigns warrant them)

## Industry-layer examples (tailor to the account)
- B2B SaaS: negate "review", "vs", "alternative", "app", "platform", "tool" only as phrase where they pull researchers (test against conversions first; over-negating suffixes can block buyers)
- Professional services: student, "pro bono", volunteer, nonprofit (if not a target), "near me" (if national)
- Agencies / consultancies: jobs, salary, "how to become", course, certification
- Hardware / physical: "second hand", used, refurbished, rental, repair (if you sell new)

## Match-type rules
- Negative broad: words to eliminate entirely regardless of query (jobs, free, cheap)
- Negative phrase: related query groups (competitor names, "tutorial", "review")
- Negative exact: one specific long-tail variation, without nuking similar queries

## Maintenance
- Mine the search terms report weekly (active accounts) for new waste.
- Quarterly: match-type sweep on high-impression negatives to catch over-negation.
- When inheriting an account: audit the existing negative lists BEFORE running campaigns; stale negatives from an old launch may be blocking queries that would convert today.

## N-gram method
Break search-term queries into single words and word pairs, sum spend and conversions per n-gram, and surface high-spend / zero-conversion patterns. Faster than a query-by-query scan and catches accidental blocks. Always human-review before applying.
