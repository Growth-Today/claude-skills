# Competitive Research (Google Ads, B2B)

## Auction Insights
- Use the Auction Insights report to see who you share auctions with: impression share, overlap rate, position-above rate, top-of-page rate.
- Watch impression-share trends, not single snapshots. A competitor's rising impression share on your core terms is a signal before it shows up in CPC.
- Lost impression share splits into "lost to budget" (raise budget or tighten targeting) and "lost to rank" (improve Quality Score or bid). Diagnose which before reacting.

## Competitor Conquest
- Bid on competitor brand terms in a dedicated campaign with its own budget and negatives.
- Expect lower Quality Score and higher CPC; you are not the brand the user searched. Justify on pipeline, not CPC.
- Message-match the ad to the switching motive (migration, pricing, a capability the competitor lacks) and send to a comparison landing page, not the homepage.
- Keep your own brand terms as negatives in the conquest campaign so signals stay clean.

## Watching the SERP
- The query surface is changing: a meaningful share of Google searches now trigger AI Overviews, and close-variant matching plus broad match pull in more adjacent queries. This adds auctions you participate in, including irrelevant ones, so the search terms report and negatives matter more, not less.
- A third gatekeeper (AI assistants / answer engines) increasingly sits between the buyer and the SERP. Track branded and category demand over time, not just last-click.

## Landing-page and offer intelligence
- Audit competitor landing pages and offers for the same keywords you target. The opening in 2026 is that most competitors still run repetitive, product-centred ad copy and generic homepages. Empathy-led copy + a dedicated, message-matched landing page beats it.

## What to do with it
- Feed findings into ad copy (Pain to Proof to CTA), negative lists (competitor terms), and budget allocation (defend high-impression-share core terms, probe where competitors are weak).
- Judge everything on pipeline contribution, not impression share or CTR alone.
