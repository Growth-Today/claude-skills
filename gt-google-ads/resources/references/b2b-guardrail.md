# B2B vs E-commerce on Google Ads — What Does NOT Transfer (read first)

Most Google Ads content, skills, and benchmarks online are **e-commerce/DTC**. Applying them to a B2B lead-gen account wastes budget. GT guardrail: filter every generic tactic through this. **Never import an e-commerce/DTC benchmark or tactic into a B2B account — not now, not later.**

---

1. **Judge on pipeline / cost-per-SQL — not ROAS or CPL.** E-commerce optimizes to purchase ROAS. B2B has no purchase at click; front-end CPL rewards cheap junk leads. Optimize + report on **cost per SQL and pipeline** via offline conversions (`crm-attribution.md`).
2. **No Shopping / retail PMax / feed.** Shopping campaigns and product feeds are e-commerce — out of scope. PMax for B2B is a **guarded, brand-excluded, offline-conversion-fed** tool, not a catalog engine.
3. **Respect the intent ladder.** Brand → high-intent non-brand → competitor → problem-aware → demand-gen. Don't buy broad/demand-gen before high-intent proves it converts to *pipeline*. E-commerce can run broad on impulse demand; B2B can't.
4. **Broad Match + AI Max require offline conversions.** In e-commerce, broad + Smart Bidding on purchase value works out of the box. In B2B it only works fed with ECL + value-based bidding on pipeline; otherwise it finds cheap non-buyers.
5. **Conversion volume is low — protect the signal.** B2B accounts have far fewer conversions than DTC, so Smart Bidding is data-starved. Use micro-conversions/journey signals (JAB) carefully and lengthen evaluation windows; don't apply DTC "wait 3 days" rules.
6. **Benchmarks are not shared.** DTC CPCs/CPAs/ROAS bands don't apply. Use **B2B** ranges, and treat even those as conditional (intent tier, industry, geography, deal size, account maturity).

**Rule of thumb:** if a tactic's success metric is ROAS, AOV, or purchases, it's e-commerce — adapt to pipeline/SQL or drop.

**B2B SaaS tooling worth naming:** HubSpot, Salesforce (CRM + offline conversions), Dreamdata (account-based attribution).
