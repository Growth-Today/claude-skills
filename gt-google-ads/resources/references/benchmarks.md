# Google Ads B2B Benchmarks & Metrics

Benchmarks vary enormously by vertical, intent, ICP, and ACV. Use these as directional gut-checks, never as targets. The only metric that ultimately matters is whether spend produces pipeline and revenue.

## Cost benchmarks (2026, B2B)

| Metric | Range / note |
|--------|--------------|
| CPC (non-brand Search) | $4-15+; legal, finance, cybersecurity, and competitive SaaS run higher |
| CPC (brand Search) | Low (you are the most relevant result); keep separate so it does not flatter non-brand |
| CPL (Search, B2B SaaS) | $50-200+; enterprise / high-ACV can run far higher and still be profitable |
| Cost per SQL | The number that matters; only visible with offline conversion import |
| Wasted spend on audit | 20-40% of budget on unqualified clicks is typical for un-audited B2B accounts |
| Negative-keyword reclaim | A 90-day search-terms sweep routinely finds $1,000-10,000/mo recoverable |

These cost figures are industry-reported ranges, not GT-measured numbers. Treat them as directional and confirm against the specific account and vertical.

## Quality Score

- Focus only on **high-spend keywords scoring below 5** — those inflate CPC where it matters.
- Ignore QS on low-volume long-tail; it barely moves total spend.
- QS is downstream of relevance: tight ad groups + message-matched ads + dedicated landing pages.

## Smart Bidding data thresholds

| Signal | Guideline |
|--------|-----------|
| Conversions for Smart Bidding to work | ~30+/month |
| Below ~15/month | Manual or Maximize Conversions without target; gather data |
| Learning phase | Lock negatives before launch so early spend does not anchor bad signal |

## Search terms / negatives cadence

| Account spend | Search-terms review |
|---------------|--------------------|
| $10k+/mo | Weekly |
| Under $10k/mo | Biweekly |
| Any | Never less than monthly |

- Flag threshold: $50+ spend and zero conversions, or CPA > 2x account average.
- Quarterly: match-type sweep on high-impression negatives to catch over-negation.

## Attribution reality

- Google defaults to data-driven attribution in 2026. Be careful comparing to last-click history.
- A meaningful share of search terms is hidden under "other" — you never see 100%.
- The B2B buying committee is large and the cycle is long; an "assisting" campaign with few last-click conversions may still be driving pipeline. Review assisted conversions before cutting it.
- Platform-reported ROAS drifts above real P&L ROAS when PMax/broad eat brand and low-intent junk. Judge on pipeline.

## Budget allocation

| Tier | Share |
|------|-------|
| BOFU (high intent) | 50-60% |
| MOFU (comparison / education) | 25-35% |
| TOFU (informational) | 10-15%, only once BOFU is predictable |
| PMax | 15-25% of total, with guardrails, once Search is clean |

## Spend floor to learn

Most B2B teams need enough budget to clear ~30 conversions/month per bid strategy for Smart Bidding to work. Expect a learning phase before judging, and read true pipeline impact over a full B2B cycle, not in the first two weeks.
